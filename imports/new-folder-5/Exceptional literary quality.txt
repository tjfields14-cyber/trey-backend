1. Exceptional literary quality
The awarded work must demonstrate outstanding literary merit, including:
Originality and Innovation: Introducing new content, forms, or pushing the boundaries of literary expression.
Artistic Power and Truth: Portraying human conflict and contemporary life with depth and accuracy.
Mastery of Narrative: Skillful storytelling and the ability to influence contemporary style. 
2. Idealistic direction and benefit to humankind
Alfred Nobel's will specifies that the prize should go to those who "shall have produced in the field of literature the most outstanding work in an ideal direction". This is interpreted as works that: 
Further Knowledge of Humanity: Deepen our understanding of the human condition and explore profound questions.
Enrich and Improve Life: Contribute to the betterment of humanity through their insights and perspectives.
Champion Human Rights and Ideals: In recent interpretations, this has encompassed a broad range of idealistic themes and principles. 
3. Body of work and impact
Life's Work: While a specific work may be highlighted, the prize generally recognizes an author's overall literary output and its lasting impact on literature and society.
Global Recognition and Influence: The work should have resonance and impact beyond national borders, influencing a broader literary dialogue. 
4. Nomination and selection process
Qualified Nomination: Candidates must be nominated by qualified individuals, such as members of the Swedish Academy, university literature professors, or previous Nobel laureates. Self-nomination is not permitted.
Rigorous Review: The Swedish Academy, with the help of a Nobel Committee, conducts a thorough evaluation of nominated works, narrowing down the candidates to a shortlist and finally selecting the laureate through a voting process.
Secrecy: The nomination and selection process is kept strictly confidential for 50 years to ensure impartiality and prevent undue influence. 
In essence, to win the Nobel Prize in Literature, a fiction author needs to craft a body of work of exceptional literary quality that explores universal themes, contributes to the betterment of humankind, and garners the attention and respect of the international literary community, leading to a nomination and selection by the Swedish Academy based on merit and impact.
