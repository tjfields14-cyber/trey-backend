Book reviewing techniques: professional vs. reader-based
Book reviewing involves both professional and reader-based approaches, each with distinct aims and methods. 
1. Professional reviews
Aim: Provide an in-depth analysis and critique for a broader audience, often with an objective and analytical focus.
Key Techniques:
Thorough Evaluation: Critically examine various aspects of the novel, such as themes, writing style, character development, plot structure, and originality.
Contextualization: Consider the book within its genre, literary landscape, author's previous works, and potentially its broader societal or historical context.
Evidence-Based Assessment: Support claims and arguments with specific examples and quotes from the text.
Identification of Strengths and Weaknesses: Offer a balanced critique, highlighting both positive and negative aspects, and explain why certain elements worked or didn't work.
Formal Structure and Tone: Follow established guidelines for structure, tone, and sometimes length, typically appearing in publications like newspapers, literary magazines, or academic journals. 
2. Reader-based reviews
Aim: Share personal opinions and recommendations with other readers, often focusing on the reading experience and emotional impact.
Key Techniques:
Focus on Personal Experience: Discuss how the book made the reader feel, whether it was engaging, emotional, thought-provoking, or easy/difficult to read.
Relatability: Highlight characters or situations that resonated with the reader.
Comparisons: Compare the book to other works or authors, especially within the same genre, to give potential readers a better sense of what to expect.
Honesty and Passion: Express enthusiasm or disappointment openly, but ideally with a respectful tone towards the author.
Informal Structure: Often less structured and more conversational, with varying length and content depending on the platform (e.g., Goodreads, personal blogs). 
3. Common techniques used by both
Brief Summary: Provide a concise overview of the plot or main idea, introducing key elements without revealing spoilers, especially for novels where twists are crucial.
Highlight Key Themes: Identify and discuss the central ideas or messages conveyed by the author.
Evaluate Characters: Assess character development, relatability, and how they contribute to the story.
Consider Writing Style: Comment on the author's prose, tone, and language, and how they impact the reader's experience.
Formulate a Recommendation: Conclude by advising specific types of readers who might enjoy or benefit from the book, or who might find it less appealing based on its style or content. 
Summarizing a novel for a review (spoiler-free)
Focus on the Big Picture: Briefly explain the core premise, main characters, and central conflict without revealing specific plot twists or the ending.
Emphasize Themes and Messages: Discuss the underlying ideas and messages without detailing how they unfold within the narrative.
Highlight Setting and Tone: Convey the atmosphere and overall feel of the book without revealing crucial plot points.
Use Comparisons (Carefully): Mention similar works or authors to give readers a sense of the book's style or genre, but avoid comparisons that could indirectly hint at the plot or outcome.
General Impressions: Share your overall experience and how the book made you feel, keeping it vague enough to avoid spoilers, according to Medium. 
Key takeaways
Book reviews serve different purposes depending on their target audience, influencing the techniques and focus of the review.
While professional reviews tend to be more formal and analytical, reader reviews often prioritize personal experience and connection.
Regardless of the type of review, a concise summary without spoilers is essential, focusing on the broader strokes of the story and its impact rather than revealing specific events or the ending.
To effectively summarize and critique a novel, consider its key elements – plot, character development, writing style, themes, and overall impact – and express your evaluation with clarity and supporting details.
